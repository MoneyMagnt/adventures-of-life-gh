The beginning Where it started.

How it happened

From three people
to 600+. Honestly.

2023

One trip. Three people. No plan.

Zico, his close friend, and Shoddy B took one trip the whole year. No brand, no flyer, no strategy. Just a shared belief that Ghana had more to offer than most people were seeing. That one trip planted something. The question: what if more people felt this?

2024

16 strangers. One minibus. Easter weekend.

The first real group trip. 16 people in a minibus built for 15. Most of them had never met. Three days on the coast — beach, tents, music, late nights. By day three, nobody wanted to go home. The feedback came in immediately: do it again.

2025

A waterfall. A thought. A text message.

Standing at a waterfall on a solo trip, Zico looked at the landscape and thought: if I bring a group here, this changes everything. He came home and texted his people — no flyer, just a message. Everyone said yes. Asenema filled in days. Akwamu Gorge followed — 20 people, a brutal hike, a view nobody forgot. Then Keta — 33 people, three days, the biggest trip yet.

2026

Africa is next.

The January island experience sold out and introduced Adventures of Life to an entirely new audience. Now the vision has expanded: at least three African countries in 2026. Togo is first. The routes are scouted. The community is ready. This is still just the beginning.

Why the name

"I didn't want Travel with Zico. I wanted something bigger. Something that meant something every time you said it."

Adventures of Life. Whatever trip we go on — it's an adventure. And it's our life. The name makes sense every single time.

Founder Zico

The standard

Three things Zico
never compromises on.

01

The route has to earn its place.

Every destination is personally visited by Zico before it becomes a trip. If the terrain doesn't deliver the experience, it never gets on the list. No exceptions.

02

He knows your name before you arrive.

Small groups mean real hosting — not crowd management. You get actual human attention from someone who is genuinely glad you came.

03

You leave with proof it happened.

Photography and content are built into every route. You don't just experience the trip — you leave with a record of it that lasts.
